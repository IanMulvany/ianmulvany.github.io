Volunteer Staff Playbook

A procedural view for consistent handling. It does not replace subject-matter judgment.

Intake questions

  1. Is the submission complete, readable, and processable?
  2. Is it topical to an existing arXiv category or cross-list?
  3. Is it a self-contained scholarly research contribution?
  4. Does the submitter appear to have rights to submit and grant the selected license?
  5. Is it a duplicate, replacement, translation, withdrawal, or related-work case?
  6. Are there conduct, authorship, privacy, or legal concerns requiring escalation?

Action ladder

  1. Return for correction when the issue is format, metadata, missing files, or PDF machine readability.
  2. Reclassify or adjust cross-lists when the content is suitable but category placement is wrong.
  3. Request consolidation or versioning for related, duplicate, or replacement-like submissions.
  4. Decline when the work is out of scope, non-scholarly, not original, or not of archival interest.
  5. Escalate rights, authorship, conduct, post-announcement, and severe offensive-content cases.

Communication boundaries

  1. Do not provide peer-review feedback on the submission.
  2. Keep unannounced submissions confidential.
  3. Route author communication through support or moderation email, not direct moderator contact.
  4. Appeal decisions can be final and may not include feedback.
  5. Post-announcement actions should preserve the historical record wherever possible.
