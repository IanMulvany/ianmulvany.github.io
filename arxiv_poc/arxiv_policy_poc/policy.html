Policy Decision Table

A working matrix for authors and staff. It separates the rule, likely action, and escalation point.

Issue Rule to apply Usual action Escalate when Primary source
Wrong category or cross-list Post in the most relevant category; cross-lists may be added or removed. Reclassify Category judgment is disputed or a procedural issue is raised. Moderation
Out of scope Submissions may be declined when arXiv does not serve the topic or intended community. Decline Boundary case across subject areas or new community coverage. Moderation
Weak scholarly form Submissions must follow accepted standards of scholarly communication and be self-contained. Decline or return Issues are fixable format problems rather than content concerns. Format policy
No original or substantive research Course projects, proposals, news, political-cause material, and work needing significant revision may be declined. Decline Publication status is raised as an appeal factor. Moderation
Duplicate or similar submission Multiple similar submissions may need consolidation or versioning. Consolidate A proxy submits a duplicate of an author-owned paper. Versions
Rights or copyright problem Submitter must have legal authority to grant the selected license, which is irrevocable. Escalate Publisher proof, barred third-party content, referee comments, or removal request appears. Licenses
PDF not machine readable PDFs must support text extraction, accessibility, and reliable rendering. Return Document is scanned, rasterized, font-broken, or JavaScript-bearing. PDF help
TeX package or file failure Authors must include required files and compatible figures; arXiv does not provide missing local style files. Return Compiler logs suggest source incompatibility or unsafe embedded content. TeX help
Generative AI language tools Significant use should be reported consistent with subject standards; AI tools must not be listed as authors. Correct metadata or text AI-generated text causes plagiarism, bias, errors, misleading content, or authorship confusion. Moderation
Conduct violation Respectful participation is required across arXiv channels. Enforce Abusive communication, repeated inappropriate material, legal concern, or account-level action. Enforcement

Editorial Calls Made In This Redesign

These choices resolve local documentation messiness without changing the underlying policy intent.

Duplicate endorsement pages

The two endorsement HTML files differ only in saved-page assets and navigation state, so the new site treats them as one source.

License wording

The license page is used for license choice and irrevocability. The submittal agreement is used for representations, warranties, and Cornell legal terms.

Moderation versus peer review

The policy guide states plainly that arXiv moderation is not peer review, while still showing moderation criteria and actions.

Author versus staff language

Rules are rewritten into author tasks, policy statements, and staff actions so the same rule is not repeated in conflicting voices.
