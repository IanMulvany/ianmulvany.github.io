R2R Conference Workspace

AI-generated session artifacts and visual experiments

This folder collects outputs created during the Researcher to Reader conference programme. Use this homepage as a quick way to browse files, compare versions, and jump into each artifact.

Top-level artifacts

These are standalone interactive pieces produced from conference discussions about responsible AI in publishing. They are designed to be quick to explore, but each one maps to real policy themes such as disclosure, accountability, authorship, confidentiality, and verification.

  • ai_use_game.html - "Reviewer #2: AI Panic Mode" is a fast, scenario-driven game where you balance integrity, transparency, and deadline pressure. Each round gives you realistic author decisions and shows how "quick AI shortcuts" can create policy or ethics risks if you skip disclosure or verification.
  • ai_use_policy_analysis.html - "AI Use-Case Permission Map" is a side-by-side matrix comparing six common AI use cases across Taylor & Francis, Wiley, and STM guidance. It is useful when you need a practical "can I do this?" view before writing, revising, or submitting.
  • Claude artifact: The Peer Review: AI Edition - The Peer Review: AI Edition is a quick, fun quiz game that tests your knowledge of responsible AI use in academic publishing. Styled like a vintage scholarly journal, complete with approval stamps, streaks, and editorial rankings, it puts you in the hot seat with 12 scenario-based questions drawn from real industry guidelines. You'll meet fictional researchers making genuinely tricky decisions: the doctor who let ChatGPT draft half their paper, the peer reviewer who outsourced their review, the chemist who faked their results with an image generator. Your job is to judge whether they've crossed a line, and how badly. Along the way you'll pick up practical knowledge of the STM Association's 9 AI activity classifications and Wiley's disclosure principles, the actual frameworks that publishers use today. Each answer comes with a clear explanation and a pointer to the relevant guideline, so you're learning as you play rather than just being told you got it wrong. Work your way up from Junior Reviewer to Editor-in-Chief (or, if you're feeling smug, Distinguished Fellow). Takes about 5-7 minutes to complete.

Guidance collection: codex_ai_use_guidance/

This subdirectory is an "AI guidelines visual studio": ten different visual interpretations of the same core publishing guidance. Instead of changing the policy message, each page changes the presentation style (checklist, cards, flow map, comic, dashboard, constellation, and more) so you can test which format helps people understand and remember the rules.

The core message across the set is consistent: disclose AI use clearly, keep humans accountable for authorship and judgment, verify outputs and citations, and protect confidential content.
